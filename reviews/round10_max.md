# Review: Round 5
**Reviewer:** Max, 3rd-year Mechanical Engineering, ETH Zurich
**Date:** April 2026
**Previous ratings:** 6 -> 7 -> 8 -> 9/10

---

## Rating: 10/10

This is the first time I have nothing structural left to complain about.

## Best new addition

The coefficient tracker table in Section 13. Seeing all nine versions of the studying coefficient in one place -- 10.5, wrong CI, seat number, wrong shape, memorised, destroyed, wrong sign, finally correct, correct by design -- is the single most useful summary in the entire document. It turns the whole course into one story with a punchline. I would print that table and tape it to my desk.

## Is anything overdone?

Honestly, it is getting close. The Coefficient Auction, Stand Up If Significant, Name That Instrument, Predict Before You Peek, the ghost callbacks, the Ofqual cold open and callback -- that is six distinct audience-participation or running-gag devices layered on top of the Toy Stories and Key Insight boxes. In a live lecture, a good instructor picks three or four and lets the rest breathe. On paper, they all work individually, but stacked together the density of coloured boxes per page is high. Not a dealbreaker -- just worth being aware of during delivery.

## One remaining thing

The "How to Read These Notes" page at the front should mention the coefficient tracker explicitly. A student skimming the intro has no idea that thread exists, and it is now arguably the backbone of the whole narrative. One sentence: "Watch the coefficient tracker notes at the start of Sections 3--10 and the summary table in Section 13."
