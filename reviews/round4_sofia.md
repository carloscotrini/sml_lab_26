## 5. My Dream Addition

If I could add ONE thing that would make me go "OMG I need to screenshot this," it would be this:

**A "Statistical BS Detector" cheat sheet at the very end.** Not a diagnostic table -- those exist and are boring. I mean a one-page guide titled something like "How to Call BS on Any Study You See on Instagram" with entries like:

- **Someone says "X causes Y"** --> Ask: what did they control for? Could there be a collider? (Section 8)
- **Someone says "significant result"** --> Ask: is this a seat number? What is the effect SIZE? (Section 5)
- **Someone says "we controlled for everything"** --> Ask: did they control for something post-treatment? (Section 8, FWL)
- **A headline says "coffee/wine/chocolate is good for you"** --> Ask: what is the hidden lifestyle confounder? Sign the bias. (Section 3)
- **Someone says "the model explains 97% of the variance"** --> Ask: on what data? Training or test? (Section 7)
- **A study has 500,000 participants and finds p < 0.001** --> Ask: what is the actual effect? 0.003 points? (Section 5)

This would be the most screenshot-able, shareable, put-it-on-your-Instagram-story page in the entire document. Every student would photograph it. It turns the entire course into a portable superpower. And it would be the thing I bring up at dinner: "My stats course literally gave us a cheat sheet for calling BS on any study."

Right now the notes teach you HOW to think. This page would teach you WHEN to deploy that thinking in the wild. That is the gap.
