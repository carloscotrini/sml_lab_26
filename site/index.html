Regression Autopsy

Eight interactive investigations into the ways ordinary least squares can mislead you — and the statistical tools that set things right. Every plot is live. Every number is computed in your browser. Drag a slider and watch your model break.

Investigations

01
The Coefficient Illusion
Your OLS coefficient looks precise and significant — but it is estimating the wrong quantity because an omitted confounder has twisted the regression surface.
01b
Controlling For
Adding a control variable should fix omitted-variable bias — unless you control for a collider or a post-treatment variable and introduce bias that was never there.
02
The Confidence Interval Delusion
Your 95% confidence interval promises coverage — but heteroscedasticity makes the actual coverage far lower than you think.
03
The R-squared Trap
A high R-squared feels like validation, but adding a post-treatment variable inflates it while destroying every causal interpretation your model had.
04
The p-Value Mirage
A tiny p-value screams significance, but when you run thousands of hypothesis tests the false-discovery rate explodes — unless you correct for multiplicity.
05
The Prediction Paradox
Your model predicts beautifully on training data yet fails catastrophically out-of-sample because it memorized noise instead of learning signal.
06
The Residual Blind Spot
Residuals look random at a glance, but structured patterns reveal model misspecification that summary statistics cannot detect.
07
The Influence Earthquake
A single data point can move your entire regression line — leverage and Cook's distance reveal which observations hold outsized power.
08
The Redemption
After seven disasters, assemble every diagnostic and repair into a principled modelling workflow that earns the right to trust its output.
