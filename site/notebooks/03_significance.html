Why Your Significance Is Wrong

Statistical significance is not the same as importance. With enough data, everything is significant—even noise.

Trap

The Regression Table

We generated 50,000 home sales from a known data-generating process and ran an OLS regression of sale price on all available variables. Every coefficient is statistically significant at \(p < 0.001\).

Reveal

Statistical vs Economic Significance

Look at the rightmost column. A variable can be wildly significant in a statistical sense yet economically irrelevant.

Zestimate has \(p < 0.001\) but its coefficient is only $3 per Zestimate-point—economically meaningless for a housing market. Meanwhile, Bedrooms adds $47,200 per bedroom but can be statistically insignificant at smaller sample sizes.
Destroy

The \(n\)-Bomb: Everything Becomes Significant

Drag the slider. The coefficient stays flat, but the standard error shrinks as \(1/\sqrt{n}\), inflating the t-statistic.

The dashed red line at \(t=1.96\) marks the conventional 5% threshold. With \(n=50{,}000\), even a $3 effect crosses it easily.

Limit

The Other Side: Type II Error

With small \(n\), even large effects can be statistically insignificant. Use the slider to see how the Type II error rate (\(\beta\)) changes.

Absence of evidence is not evidence of absence. Failing to reject \(H_0\) does not mean the effect is zero—it may mean you lacked the power to detect it.

In GWAS, researchers test ~1 million genetic variants for association with a trait. At \(\alpha=0.05\), you expect 50,000 false positives from pure noise. The Bonferroni correction sets \(\alpha^* = 0.05/1{,}000{,}000 = 5 \times 10^{-8}\).

Below: we ran 1,000,000 t-tests on pure noise (\(\mu=0, \sigma=1\)). The histogram of p-values is perfectly uniform, yet thousands fall below 0.05.

The red line marks \(\alpha=0.05\). The green line marks the Bonferroni-corrected threshold \(5 \times 10^{-8}\) (not visible at this scale—because it works).

You know what is significant and what matters. But is your model even the right shape?
